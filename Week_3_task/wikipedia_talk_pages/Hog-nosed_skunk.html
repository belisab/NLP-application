Only one species of Hog-nosed skunk?

[edit]

It appears that recent work[1] has established that the western and eastern hog-nosed skunks are the same species, and that C.leuconotus (Eastern Hog-nosed Skunk) is the correct name. Should we redirect pages, and if so, to where? Dionyseus 23:00, 14 February 2007 (UTC)[reply]

western hog nosed skunk

[edit]

I am a smoker and have recently discovered that I have a collection of at least four Hog Nosed Skunks that love to feed in my backyard. My presence does not phase them. As I walked to the edge of my deck just last night I stopped at the edge of the stairs that lead to my yard I looked down as I exhailed from a puff and not three feet from me on the second stair of three was one of the skunks. I backed up slowly anyway. I know they are hognosed because they are digging up my yard all night long, in addition to the huge all white tails. My deck was built over an existing concrete set on stairs which over time the soil underneath has washed away leaving a perfect den. I have not risked crawling under my deck to check. Can I make any observations that may be helpful to anyone? It seemed to me that not much is known specifically about the hognosed skunk. I was also wandering if anyone has heard of people getting the skunks to interact with them, if only to walk up sniff and walk away. I truly believe most animals can sense ill intentions of predators. I just want to know if I would be warned before sprayed? anyone out there care about skunks?
