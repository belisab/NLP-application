Were the mammalian reptiles lucky and unlucky ??? during the epochs

[edit]

Were the mammalian reptiles lucky and unlucky ??? during the epochs? Not exactly. The mammalian reptiles evolved the correct proportion of gene switches and transcription factors that would create:

1. a steady stream of new small species per epoch 2. a faster adaptation rate at larger orders of magnitude

So the mammalian reptiles weren't genetically superior. Neither exists a perfect recipe, merely some best suiting recipies for a specific environment (watch Harvard lectures on YouTube).

During the early Triassic the mammalian reptiles were faster at adapting to the new environment, but this speed doesn't allow maximum quality. During great dyings that speed though is most important.

We must statistically test the positioning of gene switches and transcription factors on the bird vs the mammalian DNA. Usually it's not an issue of numbers, but of subtle nuts and bolts = DNA mechanics.

What I said is absolutely moronic, because it's a hypothesis. What is important is to analyze and reveal the actual data (not to interpret them, I redesign my interpretations into new tests, and when I can't, I ask others to collect data [usually East Asians]).
