Grammar

[edit]

If this cat is named after a person (P. Simon Pallas?) or deity (Pallas Athena?) or something like that, wouldn't the correct name be "Pallas's Cat"? "Pallas' Cat" is only correct if it's named after several people/deities/misc. named Pallas. Xavius, the Satyr Lord (talk) 12:47, 19 February 2009 (UTC)[reply]

The cat is named after the naturalist, and Britannica list it as "Pallas's Cat" [1]. WP:MOS#Possessives gives both forms as acceptable for Wikipedia. We don't therefore _need_ to rename the article, although it wouldn't violate the MoS if it was renamed. Tevildo (talk) 18:03, 10 May 2009 (UTC)[reply]
On doing some checking, every other article we have on animals named after Pallas has the "s". I've proposed the move through the appropriate channels. Tevildo (talk) 18:19, 10 May 2009 (UTC)[reply]
And it's done. :) Tevildo (talk) 20:11, 10 May 2009 (UTC)[reply]

Why is it "the Pallas's cat" throughout the article? The "the" should not be there, it should just be "Pallas's cat". This seems to be the standard usage across other zoology articles where the common name is of the form "Person's animal". - UtherSRG (talk) 15:25, 10 September 2025 (UTC)[reply]

Without 'the' would imply that it would be Pallas's property. -- BhagyaMani (talk) 18:28, 10 September 2025 (UTC)[reply]
No. It is one of the meanings. Connection is another meaning of the possesive form. Being named for someone is a connection. See Przewalski's horse, for instance. UtherSRG (talk) 18:35, 10 September 2025 (UTC)[reply]
You're correct. I've removed the superfluous "the". Zacwill (talk) 09:46, 11 September 2025 (UTC)[reply]
