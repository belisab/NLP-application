Contradiction between two articles

[edit]

There is a contradiction between List of experimental cat breeds#Tiffanie and List of experimental cat breeds#Australian Tiffanie, and this article Tiffanie cat, in which the former says that Tiffanie (UK breed, a.k.a. Burmilla Longhair and other names) and Australian Tiffanie are different breeds (which makes sense or they would not be named that way), while the latter asserts they are the same breed. From what I can tell, they have similar foundation stock, but the Australian one has direct back-crossing to one of the progenitor breeds while the established (UK) Tiffanie does not. Bears some investigation, especially by someone caught up on Australian breeding and which organisations over there even still exist and what they are up to. â€‰â€”â€¯SMcCandlish â˜ Â¢â€ƒðŸ˜¼â€ƒ 04:41, 13 November 2023 (UTC); revised: 16:58, 20 January 2024 (UTC)[reply]

Discussion opened in 2023 and still no action or interest it seems. Oh well, Wikipedia is not perfect.
Although the claim in List_of_experimental_cat_breeds#Australian_Tiffanie is referenced, the reference there to Burke's Backyard is underwhelming. Don Burke is not an expert on cats so far as I can tell. The contrary claim at List_of_experimental_cat_breeds#Tiffanie (Burmilla Longhair, Asian Semi-longhair) is however unreferenced, and already flagged as such.
I propose to flag the former claim as requiring a better reference, and simply remove the latter claim as unreferenced. This is not be an ideal solution, and Wikipedia talk:WikiProject Cats has some recent activity (not a lot... but last at 8 October 2025), so I have raised the issue there too.
