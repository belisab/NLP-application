The Nominal GDP list can't sort correctly

[edit]

The Nominal GDP list uses "," instead of "." for Saudi Arabia and Turkey. This means that if you sort by nominal GDP, Saudi Arabia and Turkey are at the bottom while they should be at the top. Looking at the page: GDP (nominal) https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_(nominal) all countries use a "," I suggest replacing the "." in the GDP list with a "," to keep it in line with other pages, and fix the Nominal GDP list. Aqmery (talk) 15:26, 4 November 2025 (UTC)[reply]

I am not seeing this issue. Are your other columns sorting correctly? CMD (talk) 16:09, 4 November 2025 (UTC)[reply]
Yes, the other columns are sorting correctly.
The issue I'm seeing is that the nominal GDP list uses "." instead of "," for most of the numbers.
On the GDP(nominal) page and in the other columns in the list a "," is used, but for some reason the column with Nominal GDP (mostly) uses a "." (except for Saudi Arabia and Turkey where it's $1,083.749 and $1,437.406). Aqmery (talk) 22:19, 4 November 2025 (UTC)[reply]
