Ragdoll page editors - please read!

[edit]

May I suggest that the page be changed to put the "genetically engineered" claim more clearly. I feel I have wasted time investigating this Ann Baker and her unusual beliefs. I thought the page had been vandalised when I first saw this entry but I sought to find the truth before reverting anyone's edits!

I read this at the Iams website given as a reference.

"According to Baker, in the early 1960s the Ragdoll’s foundation cat, a longhaired white Angora look-alike named Josephine, was taken to a laboratory after being hit by a car, where she was genetically altered as part of a secret government experiment. All subsequent offspring possessed the same characteristics: non-matting fur, docile nature, larger size, imperviousness to pain and the tendency to go limp like a child’s rag doll—thus the breed's name. However, this couldn’t be confirmed, Baker claimed, since the government suppressed all the evidence.

While most well-balanced people scoff at this conspiracy theory, and genetics experts say that this kind of genetic engineering wasn’t even possible in the 1960s, this story and other Twilight Zone tales uttered by Baker have plagued Ragdoll breeders for years, since cat associations found it hard to take the breed seriously. According to the Ragdoll Connection Network, a group committed to promoting the breed, Baker’s claims became even more strange and hard to believe as time went on. For example, they say she claimed Ragdolls were crossbred with skunks to improve the cats’ tails and also represented a link between humans and extraterrestrials"

If all this X-Files/conspiracy theory stuff is from one person who offers no evidence for her assertions, then please may the page reflect this emphatically.

Respectfully Yours... --88.107.199.68 (talk) 23:43, 24 October 2009 (UTC)[reply]

Calico Ragdolls

[edit]

I was reading the sources on “ragdolls can’t be calico” and they seem rather low-quality, and one of the sources literally says that Ragdolls can be calico, and TICA allows tricolor (bicolor tortie) ragdolls, which would be considered calico. I am therefore removing the part that says they can’t be calico. Freshnarvin (talk) 01:10, 19 February 2025 (UTC)[reply]

The TICA standard is for tortoiseshell point, which is not regular tortoiseshell. [4] this is tortie point Traumnovelle (talk) 09:18, 19 February 2025 (UTC)[reply]
Bicolor Tortoiseshell point is calico point. I do not want to get into an edit war. And your sources are slop. Please do not undo my edit without proof from the breed standard and not some AI website. I do not want to get into an edit war. Freshnarvin (talk) 19:30, 19 February 2025 (UTC)[reply]
There is no such thing as 'calico point', calico is tortoiseshell and white. Traumnovelle (talk) 20:26, 19 February 2025 (UTC)[reply]
And TICA allows for tortoiseshell and white points. “Tortie Point/white” is one of the categories listed and includes tortoiseshell and white points. Calico points. Therefore the standard allows for calico cats. Freshnarvin (talk) 01:10, 20 February 2025 (UTC)[reply]
No it doesn't, you are synthesising two different things to reach an incorrect conclusion. Traumnovelle (talk) 01:21, 20 February 2025 (UTC)[reply]
My point is we are arguing semantics, and I am not the only person who considers a bicolor tortie point calico. And high white does occur despite being against breed standard, and high white + tortoiseshell is definitely calico, even if the cat also has a point coloration. Saying they don’t exist because you don’t consider calico + point calico is silly. Freshnarvin (talk) 01:34, 20 February 2025 (UTC)[reply]
Do the breed standards (or any reliable source for that matter) say they do? No. Traumnovelle (talk) 01:38, 20 February 2025 (UTC)[reply]
I’m not the one who cited AI slop articles that said calico Ragdolls do exist to prove they didn’t. A claim that they don’t exist needs a reliable source. And calico is a colloquial term. Freshnarvin (talk) 01:42, 20 February 2025 (UTC)[reply]
I actually took a closer look at the sources and they’re actually human-written slop. My bad. Freshnarvin (talk) 01:59, 20 February 2025 (UTC)[reply]
