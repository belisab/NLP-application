Untitled

[edit]

IIRC only primates have clavicles, but I'm not sure. Anyone clarify? Preacherdoc 18:32, 16 July 2006 (UTC)Preacherdoc.[reply]

To my knowledge, only animals that fly and climb do have clavicles (e.g. not animals walking on four legs). The main function for the clavicle is to keep the upper extremity hanging on the lateral side of the thorax.
/ Mats Halldin 13:21, 22 July 2006 (UTC)[reply]
Thanks for reply. I thought birds had a furcula rather than clavicles. Do bats have clavicles?Preacherdoc 21:35, 22 July 2006 (UTC).[reply]

Clavicales are only present in thosemammals who use or used their upper limbs for prehension.[1]

eytmology

[edit]

The etymology should be included.Curb Chain (talk) 03:55, 5 August 2011 (UTC)[reply]

I'm a total rookie at changing stuff on here, but "clavicula" means "tendril," according to Haubrich's Medical Meanings. He writes, "The shape of the thin, curved bone connecting the sternum and the scapula suggests the tendril of a vine." He claims that the idea that the word comes from the diminutive of "clavis" (key) is erroneous. I'm not bold enough to make the change on the page, mainly because of the citation. Drawingnearisgood (talk) 23:38, 5 October 2011 (UTC)[reply]

Proposed merge with Midclavicular line

[edit]

Could readily be merged into Clavicle # Clinical significance. This would enhance the quality of the information displayed on that page and, by virtue of providing some context, benefit readers. If there is enough content in the future, this article could be re-created LT910001 (talk) 01:10, 19 April 2014 (UTC)[reply]

Disagree with this suggested merger. Mid-clavicular line is an anatomical descriptive term, relating to an anatomical plane in the human thorax. In a physical examination the clavicle is used as the reference location point, given the ease with which it can be identified in a range of patient of different shapes and sizes. Drchriswilliams (talk) 21:45, 14 June 2014 (UTC)[reply]

Merged to Anatomical terms of location#Surface and other landmarks and the Clinical significance info to Clavicle Iztwoz (talk) 08:52, 10 August 2014 (UTC)[reply]
