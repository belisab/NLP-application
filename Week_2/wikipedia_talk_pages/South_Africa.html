As noted on the talk page of that article, comments like these appear to be based on a fundamental misunderstanding of South African institutional history, namely ignorance of the South African Constitution of 1983. The Republic of South Africa currently has a different constitution of that of the "1961 Republic" is technically true, but the current constitution did not supersede the Constitution of 1961. It superseded the Constitution of 1983, which technically resulted in a second Republic between 1983-94 that was a presidential republic with a unique tricameral parliament, as opposed to the conventional Westiminster model of the Republic from 1961-83. There wasn't a 1961 Republic that was superseded by the "Rainbow Nation" in 1994. The 1961 Republic was abrogated by the 1983 constitution, which essentially established a new Republic (the so-called "tricameral" era) which was then superseded by the "Rainbow Nation".
The 1961-83 and 1983-94 Republics had the same ruling party and adhered to minority rule, but a different framework of government altogether. --Katangais (talk) 01:05, 28 October 2025 (UTC)[reply]

The "Republic (1961-1994)" article erroneously presented South Africa as having the same constitutional system between 1961 and 1994, which it did not.
