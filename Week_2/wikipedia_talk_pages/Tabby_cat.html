"80% male"

[edit]

Many articles state as common knowledge that 80% of orange tabbies are male, but I can't find anything that seems serious enough for Wikipedia. Would be interesting if true, and if not, worth mentioning here as debunked 209.128.31.98 (talk) 16:24, 3 March 2025 (UTC)[reply]

It is already in the article and sourced to a journal article. Traumnovelle (talk) 18:58, 3 March 2025 (UTC)[reply]
If the proportion of the orange gene in a given pool is z, among N male and N female cats you should expect:
  • z N {\displaystyle zN} orange males
  • ( 1 − z ) N {\displaystyle (1-z)N} non-orange males
  • z 2 N {\displaystyle z^{2}N} orange females
  • 2 z ( 1 − z ) N {\displaystyle 2z(1-z)N} calico or tortoiseshell females
  • ( 1 − z ) 2 N {\displaystyle (1-z)^{2}N} non-orange females
If 0 < z < 1 {\displaystyle 0<z<1} , there are more orange males than orange females, but also more non-orange males than non-orange females.
The 80% assertion is equivalent to: z = 4 z 2 {\displaystyle z=4z^{2}} , whence z = 1 / 4 {\displaystyle z=1/4} . But I would expect z, like the distribution of any other trait, to vary widely by locality. —Tamfang (talk) 00:04, 7 March 2025 (UTC)[reply]
