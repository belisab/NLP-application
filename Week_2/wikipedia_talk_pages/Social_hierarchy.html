Merges

[edit]

This is the new centralised discussion regarding the overlap of social stratification, social hierarchy and social class. Previous scattered discussions can be found at #Merge and Talk:Social stratification. No consensus was reached in the previous discussions.

Everyone seems to agree on two things: (1) social stratification is a core topic of sociology; and (2) no one's ever heard the term social hierarchy used. Most of the previous disagree votes were due to this confusion. Indeed, it's social hierarchy that's problematic here; just about everything in it is covered elsewhere. All in all, this entire article can be "merged" into the other 2 and then redirected to social stratification.

My particular issue with this article (and how I reached the page in the first place) is the overlap with hierarchy and organizational hierarchy. Social hierarchies are about sociological issues, are they not? But the lead paragraph for this article doesn't do anything to distinguish that. It specifically defines a hierarchy, provides examples of organizational hierarchies, and then starts talking about social stratification in feminism without ever providing an example of a social hierarchy or even defining what social is referring to! It fails WP:LEAD by not summarizing its own contents.

Since the merge tags at "social class" and "social stratification" are now redirecting here, I'll just add one point: there's quite a bit of overlap going on between these two when there doesn't need to be. Social classes are the strata, and social stratification encompasses the entire system. The theories and issues discussed in each articles should remember that distinguishment. â€” Skittleys (talk) 04:17, 8 October 2009 (UTC)[reply]
