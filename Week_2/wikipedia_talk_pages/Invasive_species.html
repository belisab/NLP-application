Species' categorization as invasive is dependent on geography

[edit]

Given that the "invasiveness" of any given species is geographically dependent (i.e., a species is only considered invasive in a given range, not inherently invasive wherever it appears), I'm skeptical of branding a species as "invasive" without the complementary the geographic context. It would be unfortunate if a non-invasive population was impacted negatively due to readers misunderstanding the extent of a particular species' negative impacts. The lack of geographic context could also be confusing to a reader that lives in the native range of a species that is invasive elsewhere. MossOnALogTalk 18:12, 10 December 2025 (UTC)[reply]

Favorable effects section; removing content that only discusses introduced species favorable effects

[edit]

The section on favorable effects has a lot of misleading content that's really discussing introduced species that aren't considered invasive. There's already an article for introduced species, so much of this content belongs there. Since an invasive species is by definition one that has a net-negative impact on the community/ecosystem, it's illogical to discuss an invasive species as potentially having a net-positive impact. Any content that actually discusses the favorable impacts of an invasive species in a given region should be retained, such as the paragraph regarding invasive carp being used for food. MossOnALogTalk 19:12, 10 December 2025 (UTC)[reply]

The Fred Pearce book is not a reliable, neutral source and should not be used in this article as a source for claims, especially, scientific ones, when there are more rigorous studies and meta-studies to reference; the following source provides evidence of this: [1] "Taming the ‘New Wild’: tackling the good and the bad with invasive species". MossOnALogTalk 15:54, 11 December 2025 (UTC)[reply]
Here's a source showing that banana trees aren't invasive, so the discussion of indigenous groups incorporating them is not relevant to the discussion of invasive species (would potentially be appropriate for the introduced species article though). [2] "Musa". MossOnALogTalk 16:13, 11 December 2025 (UTC)[reply]
Removing the content regarding the Asian oysters, since they are not considered invasive and were not described as such by the authors of the study. [3], [4], and [5] MossOnALogTalk 16:44, 11 December 2025 (UTC)[reply]
